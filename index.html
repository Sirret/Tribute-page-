Emil Mihai Cioran

"It is not worth the bother of killing yourself, since you always kill yourself too late."

Emil Mihai Cioran (1911–1995) was a Romanian-born philosopher and essayist known for his profound pessimism and lyrical prose. His works explore themes of existential despair, futility, and the absurdity of life, often blending poetic beauty with sharp cynicism. After moving to France in the 1940s, he wrote primarily in French, producing influential works like *The Trouble with Being Born* and *A Short History of Decay*. Cioran’s writing delves into the depths of human suffering, mortality, and alienation, making him a distinctive voice in existential and nihilistic thought.

Timeline

  • 1911 — Born on April 8 in Rășinari, Romania.
  • 1928-1932 — Studied philosophy at the University of Bucharest, where he befriended Mircea Eliade and Eugen Ionescu.
  • 1934 — Published his first book, On the Heights of Despair (Pe culmile disperării), at age 23.
  • 1937 — Moved to Paris on a scholarship and began writing in French.
  • 1941 — Settled permanently in Paris.
  • 1949 — Published his first French book, A Short History of Decay (Précis de décomposition), which gained him recognition in France.
  • 1995 — Died on June 20 in Paris at age 84.
